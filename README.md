
# Lifestyle-Based-Cervical-Cancer-Risk-Prediction-
Lifestyle-Based Cervical Cancer Risk Prediction and Population Burden Analysis in West Africa

**Objective**
This project aims to analyse lifestyle-related risk factors and predict cervical cancer risk using machine learning models, while also examining the population burden of the disease across West African countries.

**Dataset Description**
The study uses a cervical cancer risk dataset containing demographic, lifestyle, and clinical features. In addition, population-level incidence and prevalence data for West African countries were analysed to assess regional disease burden.
**
Models Used**
Logistic Regression
Random Forest
XGBoost

**Key Results**
Maximum accuracy achieved: 93% (Random Forest)
Best overall model: XGBoost (after hyperparameter tuning)
Recall remained low (~0.27), indicating challenges in detecting positive cancer cases
Accuracy alone was not sufficient due to class imbalance

**Conclusion**
Cervical cancer remains a growing and unevenly distributed health burden in West Africa. While machine learning models show promise, improving recall, data quality, and early detection strategies is essential for more effective prediction and intervention.
